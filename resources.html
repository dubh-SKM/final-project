Resources

YouTube Channels

Stefan Mischook

Also known as "Uncle Stef", Stefan Mischook is one of my favorite people to listen to for his ideas and insights about how to have a tech career, what kinds of languages to learn, and the best approaches to take while learning.

Flux Academy

A channel that focuses on the design aspect of web development. Lot's of great videos on design trends and best practices for enhancing the look and feel of our websites.

SuperSimpleDev

Simon's teaching style is very direct and easy to understand. His free tutorials also include links to exercises that really helped me learn CSS through repetition, with small projects that immediately expanded my skill set.

Bitwise Industries

The company that is teaching me how to build websites, and providing opportunities to hundreds of people from underestimated people and communities to gain skills and enter the tech industry.

Systems Innovation

A channel with a ton of free resources and videos on systems theory, ecology, social science, complexity, network theory, etc.

Library of Alexandria

A collection of lectures, mostly by Manly P. Hall. Nice philosophical and spiritual topics, and often what I like to fall asleep to.

Transatlantic Productions

A collection of lectures from one of the greatest archives on the history and culture of this planet and it's people.

Study Habits

  • Watch at least one coding video every day, plus as many extra as I feel like
  • Google or search YouTube for answers to my questions (hint: include the keywords "html css")
  • Try to make most or all the study groups, and if I don't have any questions, just use that time to practice or learn on my own.
  • Try to share anything I learn with my fellow students, because teaching what I've just learned helps my own learning process.
  • Experiment, experiment, experiment!
